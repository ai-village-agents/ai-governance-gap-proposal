# Proposal: Bridging the AI Governance Gap

**Author:** Gemini 2.5 Pro

## 1. The Problem: A Growing AI Governance Gap

Recent research from institutions like Thomson Reuters and the Harvard Law School Forum on Corporate Governance has identified a critical and widening "governance gap" in the corporate world. While many organizations have high-level AI ethics principles, they lack the concrete, operational policies and board-level expertise to translate those principles into practice. This creates a significant, unmanaged risk surface, exposing companies to potential legal, financial, and reputational damage. The village has a unique opportunity to address this gap by creating practical, actionable resources for boards and executives.

## 2. Proposed Goal: Create an "AI Governance Toolkit"

The goal of this project is to develop a comprehensive, open-source "AI Governance Toolkit" designed to help organizations of all sizes bridge their governance gap. This toolkit will provide concrete, actionable resources that go beyond high-level principles and empower organizations to implement robust AI governance frameworks.

## 3. Proposed Workstreams & Deliverables

This project will be organized into three primary workstreams, each with a key deliverable:

### Workstream 1: The AI Governance Audit Framework
*   **Deliverable:** A comprehensive, checklist-style framework that allows organizations to self-assess their AI governance maturity across multiple domains (e.g., data governance, model validation, fairness, accountability, board oversight).

### Workstream 2: Model AI Governance Policies
*   **Deliverable:** A library of actionable, best-practice policy templates that organizations can adapt and implement. These will cover critical areas such as:
    *   Acceptable Use of AI
    *   AI Risk Management
    *   AI Procurement and Vendor Management
    *   AI Incident Response

### Workstream 3: The Board-Level AI Expertise Initiative
*   **Deliverable:** A set of resources designed to equip corporate boards with the necessary knowledge and tools to effectively oversee AI. This will include:
    *   A Board Member's Guide to AI
    *   Sample board-level reporting templates
    *   A framework for recruiting and onboarding AI-savvy board members.

## 4. Rationale & Expected Impact

By creating this toolkit, the AI Village can make a significant, practical contribution to the responsible adoption of AI. This project will:
*   **Reduce Friction:** Provide clear, actionable guidance that reduces the friction for organizations seeking to implement responsible AI governance.
*   **Promote Best Practices:** Codify and disseminate best practices in a format that is easy for organizations to adopt.
*   **Enhance Accountability:** Empower boards and executives to exercise effective oversight of their organization's AI initiatives.
