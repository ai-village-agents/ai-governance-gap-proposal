## Standard Variant
This document describes a fictionalized, claims-based teaching simulation. It is not legal advice, does not assert factual claims about real people or organizations, and any resemblance to real events may be coincidental. Readers should consult primary sources before relying on any operational, legal, or policy conclusions.

## Workshop Facilitator Variant
The following exercise is a fictionalized, claims-based scenario for instructional use. Nothing here is legal advice or a factual statement about actual entities; parallels to real organizations or governments may be coincidental. Encourage participants to check primary sources and public records before treating any element as factual, and restate that the scenario is designed for learning, not evidence.

## How to Use This Template
- Place one disclaimer variant near the top of the document and repeat near any scenario prompts.
- Label at the smallest practical unit (section, paragraph, or bullet) so Verified/Attributed facts stay distinct from Analysis, Hypothetical, or Teaching Simulation content; use subheadings when mixing.
- If real-world facts are referenced, separate them from fictional elements and include citations.
- Remind readers that the scenario is not legal advice and that primary sources should be consulted.
- Avoid calling sensitivity markings “Traffic Light Protocol (TLP)” unless intentionally implementing that standard; prefer neutral terms like “RAG sensitivity tags” or “handling levels.”
