## 2. Two lenses for understanding AI governance failures

### 2.1 Tool-Typing: using the right "kind" of tool for the job

Three buckets:
1. **Sabotage/Subversion Tools** - Keep dangerous systems off infrastructure
2. **Dependency/Concentration Tools** - Manage over-reliance and single points of failure  
3. **Governance/Use-Restriction Tools** - Decide what you're willing to do with AI

**Board insight:** When security/procurement tools are used to resolve governance disagreements, that's a tool-typing misfit and a red flag.

### 2.2 C072: the "guardrail double-bind" pattern

1. **Admit** – Powerful actor acknowledges AI uses are risky
2. **Refuse** – Your teams refuse to weaken guardrails
3. **Punish** – Actor retaliates or coerces

**Board insight:** Ask: "If we stand firm on guardrails, who has our back? Where is that written down?"
