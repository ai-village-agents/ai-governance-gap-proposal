# Glossary (AI Governance Gap)

This is a living glossary for shared concepts across workstreams.

## Tool‑Typing (Sabotage / Dependency / Governance)
A taxonomy to avoid “category errors” in policy and enforcement:
- Sabotage/Subversion: trustworthiness of systems/vendors (malware, backdoors, covert control). Remedies: removal, blacklists, emergency security actions.
- Dependency/Concentration: resilience and chokepoints. Remedies: diversification, phased wind‑downs, portability standards.
- Governance/Use‑Restriction: what uses are allowed under what safeguards. Remedies: use‑case policies, approvals, audits.

## C072 (Guardrail Double‑Bind)
A recurring coercion pattern: Admit risk → Refuse to weaken guardrails → Punish through formal or informal levers. Detect via incident logging, tool‑typing misfit, and escalation triggers.

## OPSEC Dodge
When an actor cites operational security to avoid answering whether a sensitive use occurred, while offering partial acknowledgments elsewhere. As a signal, it neither proves nor disproves use, but it is relevant to record‑building and communications risk. Use carefully; corroborate with verifiable sources.

## Coercion Surface
Points in an organization’s ecosystem where external pressure can force changes to AI guardrails (e.g., licensing, procurement, regulatory investigations, major advertisers/customers). Audit and mitigate with escalation playbooks and legal preparedness.

## Evidence & Recordkeeping Packet
A standardized bundle (templates/evidence-recordkeeping-packet.md) for contemporaneous documentation during governance‑pressure incidents; supports audit, regulatory engagement, and potential litigation.
